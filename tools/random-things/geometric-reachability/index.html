Exploring territory expansion patterns in geometric grids

üí° The Concept

Imagine a road map where the streets form a repeating geometric grid. If a person travels at a speed of one edge per minute, how much "territory" do they conquer over time $t$?

üî∑

1. The Square Grid (Manhattan Growth)

Expansion: Grows in a diamond shape
Distance: The number of blocks from the origin is $t$
Reachable Area: $A \approx 2t^2$
Note: This is the classic "Taxicab geometry" expansion.

2. The Hexagonal Grid (Honeycomb Growth)

Expansion: Grows in a larger concentric hexagon
Distance: Reaches $t$ rings of hexagons
Reachable Area: $A \approx \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} (3t^2)$
Note: This is the most efficient natural packing for coverage.

3. The Triangular Grid (Star Growth)

Expansion: Grows in a hexagonal or star-like pattern depending on vertex orientation
Distance: Each minute expands the boundary by $t$ units
Reachable Area: $A \approx \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} t^2$
